## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Major energy companies are deploying AI across their operations, but **procurement-specific AI is still early-stage for most**. The biggest players (Equinor, Shell, BP) are generating headline savings from operational AI (predictive maintenance, seismic analysis) — but procurement transformation lags operations by 2-3 years. Vattenfall is the most advanced on procurement AI maturity. This creates a clear window for Manu Forti.

**Key Figures:**
- Equinor: **$130M AI savings in 2025**, NOK 1B+ projected from robotics
- Shell: Automating contract authoring and supplier compliance checks
- Vattenfall: Engaged Hackett Group for AI-guided procurement transformation
- TotalEnergies: 100,000+ suppliers, $31B spend — AI at scale is a strategic imperative
- Hackett Group (March 2026): **AI use in procurement nearly doubled YoY**; workloads up 8% while headcount declines

## 1. EQUINOR (Norway) ⭐ Most Relevant Peer

### What They're Doing
Equinor published their 2025 AI results on January 7, 2026 — one of the most transparent disclosures in the sector.

**Operational AI (where savings are):**
- **$130M total AI savings in 2025**
- **$120M since 2020** from predictive maintenance: 24,000 sensors monitoring 700+ rotating machines across all facilities — predicts failures before they happen
- **$12M saved** on Johan Sverdrup Phase 3 well planning — AI found a solution human engineers hadn't considered
- **2M sq km of seismic data** interpreted using AI in 2025 — 10x faster than manual

**Procurement/Supply Chain AI:**
- Primarily still in operational/maintenance procurement (spare parts, rotating equipment)
- 100+ new AI use cases identified — procurement is on the roadmap but not yet the headline
- Focus on **Norwegian Continental Shelf 2035 ambition** — AI is "crucial"

**Quote (Hege Skryseth, EVP Technology, Digital & Innovation):**
> *"AI is a central part of our operations. Moving forward, AI will become even more important for solving industrial tasks safely, faster, more profitably, and at scale."*

**Strategic Read for Statkraft:** Equinor is ahead on operational AI but procurement-specific capability is not yet public. As a direct peer in Norway, Equinor's trajectory sets the benchmark. If Statkraft can build procurement AI capability now, it positions ahead of where Equinor will be in 2-3 years.

## 2. SHELL

### What They're Doing
Shell's AI procurement strategy is more mature on the **contract and compliance** side than most peers.

**Specific Procurement AI Initiatives:**
- **Automated contract authoring** — AI writes first drafts of procurement contracts
- **Supplier compliance checks** — AI monitors supplier obligations and flags deviations
- Goal: Reduce manual effort, shorten cycle times, mitigate legal/safety risk
- Frees up procurement professionals to focus on **strategic supplier relationships and supplier diversity**

**Technology Platform:** Shell has invested heavily in SAP ecosystem + custom AI layer
- Seismic analysis using AI (99% data reduction in processing)
- AI integrated into core procurement workflows, not just as an add-on tool

**Key Insight:** Shell is using AI to **embed intelligence into procurement workflows** — not just analytics dashboards. This is a step change from where most energy companies are.

**Relevance:** Shell's contract automation capability is 2-3 years ahead of most renewables players. The methodology is transferable — Statkraft's HV equipment contracts are a direct analogue.

## 4. BP

### What They're Doing
BP's AI strategy focuses heavily on workflow digitalization with procurement as a key workstream.

**Procurement AI:**
- **Workflow optimization** — AI agents routing purchase requests, checking compliance, automating approvals
- Positioned around "optimising BP's workflows" — procurement is a specific named workstream
- Partnership with major tech vendors (AI platforms embedded in SAP Ariba)

**Key Context:** BP is under cost pressure post-2020 restructuring — every efficiency gain matters. Procurement AI is being used as a cost reduction lever, not just a capability building exercise.

TotalEnergies has the **largest procurement challenge** in the sector.

**Scale:**

- Partnership with Saft (TotalEnergies subsidiary) investing in battery supply chain AI

**Relevance:** TotalEnergies' scale means they will build/buy sophisticated procurement AI. They're not there yet on procurement specifically — but the budget is available.

## MARKET CONTEXT: THE MACRO PICTURE

### Hackett Group 2026 Procurement AI Report (Published March 17, 2026 — TODAY)

Key findings from the **2026 Procurement Key Issues Study**:
- **AI use in procurement nearly doubled year-over-year**
- Procurement workloads projected to **increase 8% in 2026** while headcount and budgets decline
- "Leading teams are extending their impact beyond cost reduction to improve speed, insight, and outcomes"
- Trend: Moving from **AI pilots to agentic performance** (AI agents that act, not just analyse)
- **34% efficiency gains and 23% cost savings** documented from AI-driven procurement outsourcing providers

### Gartner (2024-2025)
- **74% of procurement leaders say their data isn't AI-ready**
- GenAI can automate **50-80% of current procurement work**
- Explainable AI for category strategies is the 2025 focus

### Market Gap
Energy companies are:
✅ Advanced on: operational AI, predictive maintenance, seismic/drilling AI
⚠️ Developing: contract automation, supplier compliance AI
❌ Lagging on: **category strategy AI, supplier evaluation intelligence, procurement advisory AI**
