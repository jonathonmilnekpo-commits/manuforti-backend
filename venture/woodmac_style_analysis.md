## Executive Summary

Wood Mackenzie is a Veritas-owned energy research and consulting firm that produces high-end market intelligence reports. Their visual style is professional, authoritative, and data-driven — positioning them as the "gold standard" in energy sector analysis. Product 1 should adopt elements of this style to convey similar credibility.

---

## Brand Identity Elements

### Color Palette
- **Primary: Cobalt Blue** — Deep, authoritative blue (similar to #0047AB or professional navy)
- **Secondary: White/Light Gray** — Clean backgrounds, minimalist aesthetic
- **Accent: Energy-sector colors** — They use color-coded graphics for different sectors (oil=black/dark gray, renewables=green, gas=blue, etc.)
- **Professional restraint** — Limited color palette, no bright or playful colors

### Typography (Inferred from Report Standards)
- **Sans-serif fonts** — Clean, modern readability (likely Helvetica, Arial, or custom corporate font)
- **Strong hierarchy** — Large titles, clear section headers, readable body text
- **Consistent sizing** — Predictable text scaling throughout documents

---

## Report Structure & Layout Patterns

### 1. Cover/Title Slide
- Large, bold report title
- Subtitle with date and author credentials
- Minimal imagery — often a single evocative photo or clean graphic
- Wood Mackenzie logo prominently placed
- Professional, understated authority

### 2. Executive Summary
- **"Key Findings" or "At a Glance" section**
- Bullet-pointed insights (not paragraphs)
- Strategic use of **callout boxes** for critical data points
- **Infographics** replacing text where possible
- Visual hierarchy: Most important insight = largest/most prominent

### 3. Data Presentation Style

#### Charts & Graphs:
- **Line charts** — Simple, clean lines, minimal gridlines
- **Bar charts** — Horizontal preferred for comparisons, subtle gradients or solid fills
- **Color coding by sector** — Consistent across all reports (e.g., always blue for power, black for oil)
- **Data labels directly on charts** — No legend hunting required
- **Source attribution** — Small but clear "Source: Wood Mackenzie" on every chart

#### Tables:
- Clean, minimal borders
- Alternating row colors (subtle gray/white)
- Bold headers
- Right-aligned numbers, left-aligned text
- Consistent decimal places and units

### 4. Infographics
- **"Horizons" series** uses heavy infographic style — this is their premium thought leadership
- Icons paired with data points
- Flow diagrams for processes or timelines
- Geographic maps with data overlays
- Minimal text, maximum visual information density

### 5. Section Headers
- Consistent header bar across all pages (likely with brand color)
- Section numbers (1., 2., 3.) for clear navigation
- White space around headers for breathing room

---

## Key Design Principles to Adopt for Product 1

### 1. Professional Restraint
- **Less is more** — Don't overcrowd slides
- White space is a feature, not empty space
- Every element must earn its place
- No clip art, no stock photos, no decorative flourishes

### 2. Data Authority
- Every chart needs a source attribution
- Consistent color coding across the entire deck
- Numbers should be precise and formatted consistently
- Use professional chart types (no 3D effects, no pie charts for complex data)

### 3. Visual Hierarchy
- Most important insight gets the most visual weight
- Use size, color, and position to guide the eye
- Consistent placement of similar elements (e.g., all charts have source note in bottom-right)

### 4. Executive-Ready Formatting
- Assume the reader is time-poor and senior
- Front-load the insights
- Supporting detail comes later or in appendices
- One idea per slide (or one related set of ideas)

## Specific Recommendations for Product 1 v3

### Color Scheme Update
Consider evolving from the current bright colors to a more professional Wood Mackenzie-inspired palette:

**Option A: Wood Mackenzie Style**
- Primary: Deep Cobalt Blue (#0047AB)
- Secondary: Dark Navy (#003366)
- Accent: Energy Green (#2E7D32) for positive/sustainability
- Neutral: Light Gray (#F5F5F5) backgrounds
- Text: Near-black (#212121), not pure black

**Option B: Hybrid (Keep Some Product 1 Identity)**
- Keep current Product 1 Blue but deepen it
- Add Wood Mackenzie-style professional restraint
- Use consistent sector color coding

### Layout Improvements

1. **Title Slide:** Cleaner, more whitespace, single powerful image or clean graphic
2. **Executive Summary:** Add an "At a Glance" sidebar with key metrics (revenue, risk rating, recommendation)
3. **Financial Health:** Simpler line charts — remove gridlines, add direct data labels
4. **Market Position:** Horizontal bar chart (already done) but with cleaner styling
5. **Risk Matrix:** Make it cleaner, more professional, less "PowerPoint default"
6. **All Charts:** Add source attribution line

### Typography Suggestions
- Use a clean sans-serif throughout (Calibri, Arial, or similar)
- Establish clear hierarchy:
  - Slide titles: 32-36pt, bold
  - Section headers: 18-24pt, bold
  - Body text: 12-14pt, regular
  - Captions/sources: 9-10pt, italic

### Elements to Avoid (Wood Mackenzie Don'ts)
- ❌ Bright, playful colors
- ❌ 3D chart effects
- ❌ Clip art or decorative graphics
- ❌ Busy backgrounds or gradients
- ❌ Inconsistent fonts or colors
- ❌ Paragraphs of text without visual breaks
- ❌ Missing data sources

## Benchmark Examples

### What Wood Mackenzie Does Well:
1. **"US Solar Market Insight"** — Clean data tables, clear trend lines, professional authority
2. **"Horizons" series** — High-end infographics, thought leadership positioning
3. **"Energy Transition Outlook"** — Multi-scenario forecasting with clear visual differentiation

### What Product 1 Should Emulate:
- The confidence of their data presentation
- The cleanliness of their layouts
- The consistency of their visual language
- The senior-executive-targeted tone

### What Product 1 Should Differentiate:
- Wood Mackenzie reports are dense, detailed, and expensive ($10K+)
- Product 1 should be concise, accessible, and affordable (€99-149)
- Product 1 can be more visual and less text-heavy
- Product 1 should feel "AI-powered" — modern, tech-forward
