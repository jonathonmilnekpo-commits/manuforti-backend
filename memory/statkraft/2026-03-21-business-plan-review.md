Running a parallel helprivat practice as a fastlege
What it means in practice
Fully liberal medical practice (“helt liberal legevirksomhet”) means operating without any agreement for public transfers — no driftstilskudd, no trygderefusjon — with no obligations beyond those required by law and your own private agreements (e.g. lease, employment contracts). ￼ Patients cover the full cost themselves, and nothing counts toward their frikort.

What you actually need to do
1. Register a business entity
You need a registered firm — either an enkeltpersonforetak (ENK) or AS — via Altinn (Samordnet registermelding). Most doctors starting a side practice use ENK as it’s simpler, but an AS gives liability protection.
2. Notify the municipality
The notification to the municipality should include your name, address, contact details, practice size, and information about financing (helprivat, partial refusjon, etc.). There are no specific format requirements, but it must be sent to the correct municipality. ￼
3. Notify your employer
You have a duty to inform your employer about secondary activities or engagements that could conflict with your primary employer’s interests. ￼ As a fastlege with a municipality agreement, this is important — check your fastlegeavtale for any restrictions on parallel private practice.
4. Register with NPE (Norsk pasientskadeerstatning)
Everyone providing healthcare outside the public health service is legally required under pasientskadeloven § 8 to notify NPE about their practice. This should be done no later than one week after you begin providing healthcare. ￼ You pay an annual contribution to NPE based on your activity level.
5. Set up a patient journal system
You need a compliant electronic pasientjournalsystem per forskrift om pasientjournal § 4, and must follow Normen for informasjonssikkerhet — the sector’s standard for data and privacy compliance.
6. No Helfo registration needed
Since you’re not claiming trygderefusjon, you do not need to register in Praksisinformasjon or enter into a direct settlement agreement with Helfo. ￼ That system is only relevant when you want the patient to receive state support.

Key practical considerations
Pricing: You set your own prices freely — there is no normaltariff obligation for purely private services. Outside the normaltariff, you are free to set prices yourself for services such as health certificates, insurance assessments, and similar work. ￼
Conflict with fastlegeavtalen: This is the main risk area. Your fastlegeavtale with the municipality may contain clauses limiting competing or parallel commercial activity. Worth reviewing carefully or checking with Legeforeningen’s juridical support before launching.
Separate infrastructure: You’ll likely want a separate booking system, payment solution, and potentially a separate physical space or time allocation to keep the two practices clearly distinct.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
