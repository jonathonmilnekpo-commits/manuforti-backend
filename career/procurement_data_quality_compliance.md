# Procurement Data: Ensuring Quality & Compliance
*Draft — Jonathon Milne | SVP Procurement Context*

---

## The Core Question
What data does a procurement system need to capture, track, and surface to ensure that procurement decisions are high quality and that the organisation stays compliant?

---

## 1. Supplier Data — The Foundation

Quality procurement starts with knowing who you're buying from.

**Must-have data points:**
- Supplier registration & legal entity verification (company number, jurisdiction, beneficial ownership)
- Financial health indicators (credit score, revenue, years trading)
- Compliance certifications (ISO, GDPR, anti-bribery policies, ESG ratings)
- Sanctions screening status (OFAC, EU, UN lists) — must be live/refreshed, not static
- Insurance certificates and validity dates
- Performance history (on-time delivery %, defect rates, dispute log)
- Contact hierarchy and escalation paths

**Why it matters for SVP:**
A supplier failure at scale (energy equipment, major services) can halt projects worth hundreds of millions. The data layer is your early warning system. Without it, you're reacting, not managing.

---

## 2. Contract Data — The Commitment Layer

**Must-have data points:**
- Contract value, start/end dates, renewal windows
- Key contractual obligations (SLAs, KPIs, penalty clauses)
- Payment terms and actual payment performance vs. terms
- Change order history (scope creep tracking)
- Approval chain and signatory records (audit trail)
- Auto-expiry alerts (30/60/90 day warnings)
- Linkage to POs and invoices

**Compliance angle:**
Contracts without data governance create risk. Missed renewals, unapproved extensions, and payments outside contracted terms are the most common audit findings. The system should make non-compliance the harder path.

---

## 3. Spend Data — Visibility = Control

**Must-have data points:**
- Spend by supplier, category, business unit, geography
- Contracted vs. uncontracted spend (maverick spend %)
- PO-backed vs. non-PO spend
- Spend against budget (commitment tracking)
- Invoice matching rates (2-way and 3-way matching)
- Duplicate payment detection

**KPIs to surface:**
- % spend under management
- % spend with approved suppliers
- Savings delivered vs. target (realised and pipeline)
- $/MWh, $/MW installed, $/FTE (as per your procurement vision)

---

## 4. Process & Approval Data — The Compliance Backbone

**Must-have data points:**
- Sourcing event logs (RFx history, evaluation scores, award decisions)
- Approval workflow records (who approved, when, at what threshold)
- Segregation of duties compliance (no single person raising + approving + paying)
- Delegation of authority matrix, version-controlled
- Exceptions and waivers log (who requested, who approved, reason)
- Training completion records for procurement staff

**Why this is SVP-critical:**
When the board or auditors ask "how do we know decisions were made properly?" — this is your answer. The process data IS your compliance evidence.

---

## 5. Risk Data — Forward-Looking Intelligence

**Must-have data points:**
- Supplier concentration risk (% of category spend with single supplier)
- Geographic/geopolitical exposure by supplier HQ and manufacturing location
- Single-source dependencies flagged and reviewed
- ESG/sustainability risk scoring
- Open issues and remediation status per supplier
- Business continuity plan existence and last tested date

**Energy sector specifics:**
For Statkraft: turbine/solar panel supply chains with heavy China exposure, cable and grid equipment with long lead times, and construction contractors with safety/compliance records — all need dedicated risk data capture.

---

## 6. What "Good" Looks Like — The Target State

| Metric | Current State (typical) | Target State |
|--------|------------------------|--------------|
| Spend visibility | 60–70% | 95%+ |
| Supplier data completeness | Patchy | 100% for strategic/critical |
| Contract coverage | <50% of spend | 85%+ |
| Maverick spend | 20–30% | <10% |
| Audit findings on procurement | Recurring | Zero repeat findings |
| Savings tracked & validated | Inconsistent | 100% with CFO sign-off |

---

## 7. Practical First Steps (Year 1 as SVP)

1. **Data audit** — what does the current system actually capture vs. what's missing?
2. **Supplier master data cleanse** — duplicates, inactive suppliers, missing compliance docs
3. **Spend categorisation** — if spend isn't categorised, nothing else works
4. **Contract repository** — centralise all contracts with metadata and expiry alerts
5. **Dashboard for leadership** — simple, real-time view of the metrics that matter

## Interview Talking Point

*"Quality and compliance in procurement aren't about adding bureaucracy — they're about having the right data in the right place at the right time. If our systems surface risk before it becomes a problem, flag a non-compliant supplier before we award them a contract, and give the CFO a real-time view of where our money is going — that's a procurement function operating at the level Statkraft needs."*
