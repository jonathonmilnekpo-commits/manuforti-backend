**Category Strategy is a decision-making instrument.** It transforms procurement from reactive buying into proactive, value-creating strategy. We give clients the evidence, argument, and presentation they need to make bold moves with confidence.

## The Three Questions

Every Category Strategy answers:

1. **Where are we now?** — Market reality, supplier landscape, current approach
2. **What could we do?** — Strategic options, evaluated rigorously
3. **What should we do?** — Clear recommendation with financial justification

## The Four Pillars

### 1. Evidence-Based
No opinions without numbers. Every claim backed by:
- Market data (supplier revenues, market size, growth rates)
- Pricing intelligence (benchmarks, discount structures)
- Quantified value (€ saved, risk € avoided, delay costs prevented)

### 2. Decision-Focused
Not a research report — a decision tool. The output:
- States the problem in one paragraph
- Recommends the solution in one sentence
- Justifies it with one financial summary

### 3. Rigorously Evaluated
Multiple strategic options scored objectively using:
- **AHP** (Analytic Hierarchy Process) — weights criteria by importance
- **TOPSIS** — ranks options by distance from ideal
- **Sensitivity analysis** — tests if the answer holds under different assumptions

### 4. Implementation-Ready
Includes roadmap, phases, owners, and next steps. Strategy without implementation is just a document.

---

## The Value Formula

```
Category Strategy Value = (Cost of Inaction) + (Opportunity Captured) - (Programme Cost)
```

**Cost of Inaction:** What continuing the current approach costs per year (delays, overpayments, risks)

**Opportunity Captured:** What the recommended strategy delivers (savings, risk reduction, revenue enablement)

**Programme Cost:** What it takes to implement (fees, internal time, transition costs)
